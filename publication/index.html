The Effects of Minimum Lot Sizes on Housing Markets, Neighborhoods, and Individuals
I study the effects of Houston’s 1998 subdivision reform, which relaxed land-use regulations in parts of the city. I analyze first-order effects of the reform on housing supply and housing affordability and its downstream consequences for neighborhoods. Future work will study its impacts on incumbents.
