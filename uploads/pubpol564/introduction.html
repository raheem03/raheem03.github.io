Chapter 1 Introduction

Lecture Preview

Learning Objectives

After this week, students will be able to:

  • Articulate their goals for the course, and link the structure of the course to those learning objectives
  • Reflect on what it means to have expertise in a policy domain
  • Explain why housing matters for individual well-being and society more broadly
  • Assess competing perspectives on the purpose of housing policy in the US

Readings

This week, you have three readings:

  1. Recording on learning and developing expertise
    • What is an expert?
    • What are some features of expert knowledge, and what distinguishes it from that of novices?
  2. Ramakrishnan et al, “Why Housing Matters for Upward Mobility.” I imagine you’re studying housing policy because you think housing is important for well-being. Why does housing matter for well-being? There may be many reasons, but in this article, the authors discuss the ways in which housing might matter specifically for the upward mobility of children.
    • Are there other ways housing might matter for intergenerational mobility?
    • What other outcomes might housing matter for? How?
  3. Milton Friedman, Capitalism and Freedom, Ch 2. Milton Friedman was a neoclassical economist and perhaps the most influential economist of the latter half of the 20th century. In general, he believed in minimizing government intervention and in the primacy of the free market. In this chapter, he articulates some justifications for government interventions in markets, which may help us think about the purpose of and justification for housing policy.
    • What conceptual justifications does Friedman provide for government intervention in the economy?
    • Under these conditions, to what extent could the government justify intervening in the housing market?
    • Are there other justifications for intervening in the housing market?

Discussion Questions

  1. What are your goals for this class? How might you achieve those goals?
  2. Why might housing matter for well-being?
  3. What is – or should be – the goals of housing policy?
