Chapter 3 Housing Vouchers

Lecture Preview

Learning Objectives

After this week, students will be able to:

  • Explain the history and rationale of the Housing Choice Voucher (HCV) program
  • Describe key features of how the program is designed and discuss the policy’s implications
  • Evaluate the evidence on program impacts
  • Read and critique policy-relevant academic papers

Readings

Normally, I require readings that in some way complement the core topic of the lecture. Before lecture, it’s not always obvious how the reading relates to the core topic. One reason I provide guiding questions, then, is to help focus your reading and to draw attention to specific parts of the text.

This week, I really want you to read everything very closely. For this reason, I was a little tempted not to provide questions because I don’t want you to home in on certain parts of the texts at the expense of others. I decided it would be helpful to have offer some guidance on reading the paper and some guiding questions, but just keep in mind that I want you to do a close read.

  1. Chetty et al, “The Effects of Exposure to Better Neighborhoods on Children”
    • Some tips on reading an Economics paper at your stage:
      • Start with the Abstract.
      • Then, read the Introduction very carefully. The nice thing about Econ papers is that the Introduction is usually a completely self-contained summary of the paper. It also helps alert you to what the author thinks are the most important parts of the paper.
      • Next, I’d go through the tables and figures in order. Together, these usually convey the argument the authors are trying to make.
      • Then, I’d go back and read the rest of the paper – the Background, Data, Analysis/Results, and any other sections (in this paper, they have a section on reconciling findings with earlier work, and a cost-benefit analysis)
    • Are there parts of the paper you really struggled with? Take a note and let’s talk about it in class!
    • Some guiding questions:
      • What was the Moving to Opportunity experiment? What were its goals? How was it designed?
      • What are the treatment and control groups?
      • How do they analyze impacts of the program?
      • Do you think their design allows them to reliably determine the causal effect of the program?
      • What outcomes do they look at?
      • What are the main results? What do you find most interesting? What else do you think they could’ve looked at?
      • They find heterogeneity in effects based on some characteristic of the children. What is the characteristic? Why do you think this heterogeneity might exist?
      • What are the results of the cost-benefit analysis? What are the policy implications?
      • Do you agree or disagree with their assessment. Why?
  2. Chetty et al, “Non-Technical Summary
    • Normally, I’d read a non-technical summary first. For this assignment, I’d like you to read it after you read the paper.
    • Think about how they frame their results and the aspects of the paper they highlight. This reflects what the authors think are the most important parts of the paper. Did you have different takeaways/highlights? Is there anything they didn’t include that you would’ve? Why do you think they included the information that they did?
    • Did this summary help clarify any confusion you had about the paper?
  3. “Overview of the Creating Moves to Opportunity Program in Seattle and King County”
    • The CMTO program was created following the results of the original Chetty at al study. How do you think the findings from the paper informed the design of this program?

The lecture is partly built on Schwartz, Ch 8. I encourage you to scan this chapter.
