Chapter 2 History; Public Housing

Lecture Preview

Learning Objectives

After this week, students will be able to:

  • Describe how US housing policy has developed over time, from the New Deal to the present
  • Identify major housing policies and policy shifts in the history of US housing policy
  • Explain reasons for government involvement in housing and articulate key tensions in debates around housing policy
  • Describe the history, design, and key features of the public housing program
  • Apply tools from microeconomics to consider effects of the program’s design
  • Apply tools from quantitative methods to assess the empirical evidence on the impacts of the public housing program

Readings

Here are the videos and readings for the week, along with some guiding questions:

  1. Watch: Adam Ruins Everything, “The Disturbing History of the Suburbs”
    • The video argues that federal housing policy actively encouraged racial segregation. Do you buy this narrative?
    • If the video’s assessment is correct, would it change how you think about what government’s role is in promoting racial integragtion today?
  2. Sampson, “Move Up or Out? Confronting Compounded Deprivation”
    • What is the difference between place-based and people-based policies?
    • What does Sampson identify as the main pros/cons of each approach?
  3. Rothstein, The Color of Law, Ch 2
    • What role did the government play in segregating public housing?
    • There were debates about whether public housing should target a broad swath of American society vs very low-income renters. Ultimately, what types of families did the program target?
    • How might the government’s decisions (to integrate vs segregate, to target middle-class vs low-income) have contributed to the perception and evolution of the program over the 20th century?
  4. Popkin, “Lessons from 40 Years of Public Housing Policy”
    • What are some causes of the public housing crisis?
    • How would you characterize the shifts in public housing policy since the 1980s?
    • Does Popkin have any recommendations on how low-income rental assistance should evolve moving forward?

The lecture is partly built on von Hoffman’s “History lessons for today’s housing policy: the politics of low-income housing” and Schwartz, Ch 6. I encourage you to scan both of these readings.
