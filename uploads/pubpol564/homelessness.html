Chapter 7 Homelessness

Lecture Preview

Learning Objectives

After this week, students will be able to:

  • Identify key housing problems related to instability
  • Consider what the goals of housing policy should be and critically think about how we should allocate limited resources
  • Evaluate the evidence on the causes of homelessness
  • Describe the major policy approaches to addressing homelessness and evaluate the empirical evidence on their impacts
  • Assess practical implementation challenges associated with different interventions

Readings

  1. Shinn and Khadduri, Ch 2
    • What are the two dominant classes of explanations for homelessness?
    • How do the experiences of homelessness and poverty differ in the US relative to other countries?
    • What do Shinn and Khadduri think best explains levels and trends in homelessness in the US? What evidence do they have to support their claim?
    • What individual risk factors do they identify as contributing to an increasing probability of experiencing homelessness? How might policy mediate the relationship between individual risk factors and homelessness?
  2. Family Options Study, 3-Year Summary Report
    • What is the Family Options Study? That is, what is the motivation behind the program, the main research questions the study seeks to answer, and the overarching research design?
    • What are the main impacts identified from the three-year follow-up?
    • What conclusions can you draw from their cost analysis?
