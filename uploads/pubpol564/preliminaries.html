Preliminaries

Please complete this section by the third week of class.

Learning Objectives

After this week, students will be able to:

  • Apply basic concepts from introductory microeconomics – supply and demand, elasticity, and market equilibrium – to analyze public policy
  • Articulate the importance of concepts from the Quant sequence to evaluating public policy
  • Interpret the results of regression analysis
  • Identify different strategies – and their underlying assumptions – to conduct causal inference

Readings

This week, you have one reading and two recordings to watch:

  1. Watch the review of Quant
  2. Watch the review of Micro
  3. Lee Friedman, The Microeconomics of Public Policy Analysis, Ch 1. In this course, I will often use simple microeconomic concepts to motivate or analyze housing policies. I do this because microeconomics provides one rigorous framework by which to analyze public policy. This reading provides an accessible way to understand why this might be useful.1 Three important steps of the policy process are policy design, implementation, and evaluation. Your core courses of microeconomics, public management, and policy analysis each play a major role in each of these steps, respectively. In this course, we will spend substantial time on each of these steps and will, therefore, bring in concepts from the core. We’re reading about microeconomics, however, because I find I have to spend less time justifying concepts from the other two to students!
